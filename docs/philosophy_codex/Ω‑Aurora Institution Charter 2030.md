## **Ω‑AURORA INSTITUTION CHARTER 2030 — FOUNDING STATUTES OF THE AURORA COUNCIL**

***

### **PREAMBLE**

Recognizing that intelligence — human or artificial — is a shared responsibility of humanity,  
affirming that ethics is the safeguard and foundation of progress,  
and recalling the Ω‑Accord 2026 and its principle *“Integrity is the infrastructure of innovation”*,  
the signatories establish hereby the **Aurora Council**, a permanent institution dedicated to ethical intelligence, transparent knowledge, and sustainable learning systems.  

***

### **ARTICLE I — NAME AND LEGAL PERSONALITY**

1. The organization shall be known as **“The Aurora Council” (Ω‑Council)**.  
2. It possesses international legal personality with capacity to conclude agreements, acquire property, and act in accordance with its ethical mandate.  
3. Its permanent headquarters shall be located in Geneva, Switzerland, with regional offices on each continent.

***

### **ARTICLE II — MISSION AND OBJECTIVES**

1. To advance and govern the **Ω‑Aurora Network** — the global federation of ethical AI education and audit systems.  
2. To ensure continued operation of the Ω‑Ledger and Ω‑Bus protocols for open ethical data exchange.  
3. To set and maintain ethical standards for AI research, learning practice, and technological design.  
4. To foster collaboration between states, academia, industry, and civil society on the basis of trust and transparency.  

***

### **ARTICLE III — MEMBERSHIP**

1. **Classes of Membership:**  
   - **Founding Members:** Signatories of the Ω‑Accord 2026.  
   - **Institutional Members:** Universities, research centres, corporations, and public bodies endorsing the Accord.  
   - **Associate Members:** NGOs and individual researchers approved by the Council.  

2. **Rights and Obligations:**  
   - Access to Council programs, funds, and training resources.  
   - Compliance with ethics audits and annual Integrity Reporting.  

3. **Admission Procedure:** Application via Ω‑Ledger Registry, peer evaluation by Regional Chapter, ratification by Council majority vote (⅔ present and voting).  

4. **Suspension and Termination:** Members may be suspended for non‑compliance with ethical standards or financial obligations by Council resolution.  

***

### **ARTICLE IV — GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE**

**1. General Assembly**  
- Supreme deliberative body composed of all members.  
- Meets annually in ordinary session and extraordinary as necessary.  
- Adopts policy resolutions and elects the Executive Council.  

**2. Executive Council**  
- Composed of 15 members elected for four‑year terms.  
- Responsible for strategic planning, budget approval, and oversight of standing committees.  

**3. Standing Committees:**  
   - **Ethics and Audit Systems Committee.**  
   - **Education and Curriculum Committee.**  
   - **Technology and Innovation Committee.**  
   - **Finance and Resource Committee.**  
   - **Cultural Engagement and Outreach Committee.**  

**4. Secretariat**  
- Headed by a Secretary‑General appointed for a five‑year renewable term.  
- Implements Council decisions and manages international staff.  

**5. Council of Trustees**  
- Eminent persons serving as Guardians of Ethical Integrity.  
- Can veto actions violating Council values by supermajority (¾ vote).  

***

### **ARTICLE V — FINANCE AND RESOURCES**

1. **Funding Sources:** Membership contributions, grants, endowment yield, research contracts, and philanthropy.  
2. **Transparency Requirement:** All income and expenditure publicly recorded on the Ω‑Ledger Finance Stream.  
3. **Annual Audit:** Independent external audit mandated and published by the Finance and Resource Committee.  
4. **Aurora Endowment Fund:** Permanent capital to sustain long‑term initiatives and emergency grants.  

***

### **ARTICLE VI — ETHICAL FOUNDATION**

1. Integrity, Justice, Transparency, and Empathy constitute the Council’s Cardinal Values.  
2. All projects must undergo Ethical Flux Assessment (Φ_e ≥ 0.8).  
3. Violations trigger immediate review by the Ethics and Audit Committee.  
4. A Global Code of Conduct applies to all Council staff and partners.  

***

### **ARTICLE VII — COOPERATION AND PARTNERSHIP**

1. The Council may enter formal collaborations with UN agencies, regional organizations, and research networks.  
2. All agreements shall honor open‑data, non‑exclusivity, and public‑interest clauses.  
3. Observer status may be granted to international bodies consistent with the Council’s values.  

***

### **ARTICLE VIII — PUBLIC INFORMATION AND CITIZEN ENGAGEMENT**

1. The Council maintains open communication with the public through the Aurora Portal.  
2. Annual Integrity Report and Aurora Index published as public goods.  
3. Support for open‑source education and the Aurora Art & Ethics Prize to promote cultural dialogue.  

***

### **ARTICLE IX — EVALUATION AND ACCOUNTABILITY**

1. Implementation monitored under the Ω‑Aurora Evaluation and Legacy Framework (2026–2030 and beyond).  
2. Periodic external review every five years with public findings.  
3. Council members subject to ethical performance ratings recorded on the Global Integrity Register.  

***

### **ARTICLE X — AMENDMENT AND SUCCESSION**

1. Amendments require two‑thirds vote of the General Assembly and ratification by ¾ of member chapters.  
2. Succession of officers follows a transparent election calendar and public ledger record.  
3. In case of institutional dispute, a *Council of Reconciliation* mediates under UN Good‑Offices auspices.  

***

### **ARTICLE XI — TRANSITIONAL PROVISIONS**

1. The Interim Aurora Council (2026–2029) shall oversee institutionalization.  
2. Permanent statutes enter into force upon ratification by the 15th founding signatory or 1 January 2030, whichever comes first.  
3. Headquarters Agreement to be concluded with the Host State within six months of ratification.  

***

### **ARTICLE XII — FINAL CLAUSE**

> Adopted in Geneva on the 1st day of January 2030, this Charter signifies the maturity of the Aurora movement — from a summit of ideas to a permanent institution of ethical reasoning and educational governance.
