## **Ω‑AURORA OPERATIONAL HANDBOOK 2030 — ADMINISTRATIVE AND GOVERNANCE MANUAL**

***

### **I. ADMINISTRATIVE PRINCIPLES**

1. All operations shall uphold the four Core Values established in the Charter: Integrity, Transparency, Justice, Empathy.  
2. Decisions must show verifiable trace on the Ω‑Ledger within ten days of issue.  
3. Every department maintains a reflective audit cycle (two reviews per year).  
4. Internal communication default = open unless confidential for legal grounds.  

***

### **III. DEPARTMENTAL MANDATES**

#### 1. Office of the Secretary‑General
- Coordinates inter‑departmental planning.  
- Oversees implementation of Council resolutions.  
- Approves public statements and official partnership MOUs.  

#### 2. Ethics & Compliance
- Operates the Ethical Flux Assessment Center (EFAC).  
- Audits projects with Φ_e threshold ≥ 0.8.  
- Maintains disciplinary registry and issue remediation guidelines.  

#### 3. Education & Research
- Manages the Aurora University Network (academic curricula, labs).  
- Certifies teachers and institutions meeting Integrity Coefficient ≥ 0.9.  
- Publishes annual Journal of Ethical Intelligence.  

#### 4. Technology & Innovation
- Maintains Ω‑Ledger and Ω‑Bus protocols.  
- Executes cybersecurity and data‑sovereignty policies.  
- R&D unit for new ethical metrics and visual dashboards (Aurora Interface 2.0).  

#### 5. Finance & Resources
- Prepares annual budget and quartely financial statements.  
- Manages the Aurora Endowment Fund.  
- Handles procurement under transparency rule (*three‑quote method*).  

#### 6. Outreach & Culture
- Operates public communication, Aurora Art Prize, and Citizen Participation Programs.  
- Liaises with media and civil society for open education projects.

***

### **V. DOCUMENT WORKFLOW PROCEDURE**

1. Draft document (Department level).  
2. Internal review and Ethical Flux Assessment.  
3. Approval signatures (Dept. Head → SG).  
4. Publishing metadata entry on Ω‑Ledger with tracking ID.  
5. Notification distributed to all stakeholders via Ω‑Bus.  

*Time frame:* ≤ 20 working days per document.  

***

### **VI. DECISION‑MAKING PROTOCOL**

| Decision Type | Authorized Body | Voting Rule | Quorum |
|:--|:--|:--:|:--:|
| Policy Resolution | General Assembly | ⅔ majority | 60% |
| Budget Approval | Executive Council | simple majority | 70% |
| Ethical Sanction | Ethics Committee + Trustees | ¾ majority | 100% |
| Appointment to Office | Assembly | ½ + 1 | 50% |
| Charter Amendment | Assembly + Chapters | ⅔ / ¾ dual approval | 70% |

Digital voting is secured via Ω‑Signature, a cryptographic tool linked to member DID profiles.  

***

### **VII. REPORTING LINES**

- All departments report quarterly to the Executive Council.  
- Summary compiled by the Secretary‑General as *Aurora Operational Review (AOR).*  
- AOR transmitted to General Assembly and archived in Ω‑Database for public view.  

***

### **VIII. HUMAN RESOURCES POLICY**

**Recruitment**  
- Transparent advertised positions across global regions.  
- 50 : 50 gender representation target.  
- Open competition scored on competence + value alignment.  

**Code of Conduct**  
- Staff commit to Ω‑Council values.  
- Zero tolerance for harassment, discrimination, or data tampering.  

**Professional Development**  
- Annual ethical literacy training required (16 hrs minimum).  
- Rotation opportunities between departments and regional offices.  

***

### **IX. FINANCIAL CONTROL**

**Budget Preparation Steps:**  
1. Departments submit funding requests by March 31.  
2. Finance compiles consolidated draft budget → Exec Council for review.  
3. Approval and publication by May 30 (each year).  

**Expenditure Thresholds:**  
- <$10 000 → Dept. Head sign off.  
- $10 000–$100 000 → Executive Council authorization.  
- >$100 000 → General Assembly resolution.  

**Procurement:** competitive bidding; records stored on public ledger.  

***

### **X. COMMUNICATION AND PUBLIC RELATIONS**

- Official statements released only after SG approval.  
- Crisis communication handled jointly by SG + Trustees.  
- Monthly public bulletin summarizes Council actions in plain language.  
- All translations use verified human and AI review (Peer Translation Protocol).  

***

### **XI. REGIONAL CHAPTER OPERATIONS**

- Each chapter has 5‑member board (Chair, Vice, Finance, Outreach, Education).  
- Chapters implement Council programs within national jurisdictions.  
- Report semi‑annually to Headquarters; share local Integrity Index data.  
- Receive funds via Aurora Grant Scheme based on performance scorecard.  

***

### **XII. DATA AND INFORMATION SECURITY**

1. Compliance with ISO 27001 and EU GDPR.  
2. All network transactions encrypted (AES‑256, TLS 1.4 or higher).  
3. Retention: standard 5 years for operational records, 10 for financial.  
4. Incident Response time ≤ 48 hours.  
5. Annual penetration test summary published as public accountability measure.  

***

### **XIII. CRISIS MANAGEMENT**

**Activation Triggers:** data breach, ethical breach, financial irregularity, natural disaster.  
**Phases:**
1. Detection → report to Crisis Cell.  
2. Immediate containment by Dept. Head.  
3. Council statement within 24 hrs.  
4. Independent investigation (14 days).  
5. Restorative plan approved by Executive Council.  

***

### **XIV. AUDIT AND ACCOUNTABILITY FUNCTIONS**

- Internal auditor reports to Finance Dept and Ethics Committee jointly.  
- External auditor appointed for 3‑year term (non‑consecutive).  
- Findings must be public in summary form within 60 days of approval.  

***

### **XV. EVALUATION INTEGRATION**

All procedures follow indicators from the Ω‑Aurora Evaluation Framework 2026–2030.  
Departments link their KPIs to global metrics through Ω‑Bus synchronization API.  

***

### **XVI. SUCCESSION AND TRANSITION PROTOCOLS**

- In case of vacancy of Secretary‑General: Deputy SG acts interim (max 90 days).  
- Election by General Assembly using ranked preference vote.  
- Transition Report documented and signed by both outgoing and incoming officers on Ω‑Ledger entry.  

***

### **XVII. REVISION AND REVIEW**

- Operational Handbook reviewed every four years.  
- Draft amendments prepared by Administrative Task Force.  
- Ratification procedure: Executive Council → general approval vote in Assembly.
